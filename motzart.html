Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart (27 January 1756 – 5 December 1791) was a prolific and influential composer of the Classical period. His extensive body of work includes over 800 compositions that are celebrated for their melodic beauty, formal elegance, and rich harmony and texture. Mozart's genius was evident from a young age, and he became famous for his virtuosic performances and profound compositions that have left a lasting legacy in the world of classical music.
